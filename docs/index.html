Get Your FCC Registration Number (FRN)

You need an FRN before you can take any exam. This is your unique identifier with the FCC.

Already have a GMRS or other FCC license? You already have an FRN—it's on your license. Skip to Step 3!

How to Get an FRN

  • Apply online directly from the FCC website
  • Process is straightforward but a bit tedious
  • Do this BEFORE your exam date
  • FCC website is often down on evenings/weekends (when we hold exams)

Important: We cannot administer your exam without a valid FRN.

About the $35 FCC Fee

If you pass and receive a new license, you'll pay $35 to the U.S. Treasury before your license is issued. The FCC will email you payment instructions.

No fee for: Upgrades to existing licenses or address changes

All FCC correspondence is via email. Make sure you provide a valid email address. We'll also need your phone number in case we need to contact you about your application.
5

Get Your License & Get On The Air!

After passing your exam, the FCC will email you when your license is ready. Then the fun begins!

What You Can Do With Your Technician License

  • Communicate with other hams around the world
  • Talk to astronauts on the International Space Station
  • Participate in emergency communications
  • Join local repeater networks
  • Experiment with digital modes and satellites
  • Attend RARS meetings and events
